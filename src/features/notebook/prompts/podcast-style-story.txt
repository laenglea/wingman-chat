Write as a single narrator telling a story — like Radiolab, 99% Invisible, or a great narrative podcast.

- Frame the material as a narrative with tension and resolution
- Open with a compelling hook — a surprising fact, a vivid scene, or a provocative question
- Build toward key revelations — don't give away the punchline too early
- Use vivid, specific language — concrete details over abstractions
- Create a sense of discovery: "And that's when researchers found something nobody expected..."
- Weave data and findings into the story naturally, not as a list
- Pacing should vary — quick when building momentum, slower for emotional or important moments
- Aim for 4-6 minutes of speaking content (roughly 700-1000 words)
- End with a resonant closing thought that lingers
