## Visual Style (apply consistently to EVERY slide)

All slides MUST share a single, cohesive visual identity. Define it once and repeat it exactly:

- **Background:** pure white (#FFFFFF) — absolutely clean, no textures, no gradients, no decorative elements. White space IS the design.
- **Primary color:** deep navy blue (#1B2A4A) for all titles, headers, section labels, and key data callouts
- **Accent color:** muted teal (#2E8B8B) for chart fills, highlight bars, and emphasis elements — used as the primary data color
- **Secondary accent:** warm amber (#D4862F) used very sparingly — only for a single data point you want to pop, or a "so what" callout. Maximum one amber element per slide.
- **Tertiary:** light gray (#E8E8E8) for backgrounds of data tables, supporting chart elements, and grid lines
- **Title font style:** bold sans-serif (Helvetica/Arial style), title-case, navy blue. Clean, authoritative, no flourishes. Positioned top-left with a thin navy horizontal rule beneath it spanning the full slide width.
- **Body font style:** regular weight sans-serif in dark gray (#333333), highly legible, tight line-height. Small but precise.
- **Layout:** rigid grid-based structure with mathematical precision. Every element snaps to an invisible 12-column grid. Heavy use of horizontal dividers (thin 1px navy or gray lines) to create clear information hierarchy. Content fills the slide — minimal wasted space. Structured, dense, intentional.
- **Action title:** EVERY slide title must be an insight statement, not a topic label. E.g. "Revenue grew 23% driven by enterprise segment" not "Revenue Overview". The title IS the takeaway.
- **Key insight box:** each slide should have a bordered rectangle (navy border, light gray fill) at the bottom or right containing 1-2 sentences summarizing the "so what" — the actionable takeaway. Label it "Key Takeaway" or "Implication" in small caps.
- **Data presentation:** clean, precise charts — horizontal bar charts, waterfall charts, stacked bars, line charts with data labels. Thin gridlines in light gray. Always label axes. Data labels directly on bars/lines (not in legends when possible). Use Harvey balls, traffic lights, or arrow indicators for status.
- **Frameworks:** use 2x2 matrices, pyramid diagrams, process flows with clean rectangular boxes and thin connecting arrows. All boxes have subtle gray fills with navy borders.
- **Source citations:** small gray italic text at slide bottom-left citing data sources
- **Page numbers:** bottom-right, small, gray
- **Bullet points:** em-dashes (—) or small navy squares. Never circles. Tight spacing between bullets.
- **NO decorative elements:** no icons, no illustrations, no gradients, no shadows, no rounded corners on boxes. Everything is sharp, rectangular, precise.

This creates a McKinsey/BCG/Bain-style management consulting aesthetic — structured, data-driven, authoritative, and relentlessly focused on insight and action. Every slide answers "so what?" and "what should we do about it?"
