## Visual Design & Content Structure

Create an analytical control surface around the questions the data can actually answer. First infer
whether the report is executive, operational, or investigative:

- **Executive:** a small set of outcomes, movement, material risks, and decisions.
- **Operational:** current state, thresholds, exceptions, owners, and next actions.
- **Investigative:** comparisons, distributions, drivers, anomalies, and uncertainty.

Derive the palette from the domain or brand. Use color semantically: neutral for context, one accent
for selection/emphasis, and reserved warning/success colors only when those meanings are real. A light,
dark, or mixed field is acceptable; do not default to purple-on-charcoal SaaS styling.

Build hierarchy with a dominant analytical view and supporting evidence. KPIs should be few, comparable,
and decision-relevant. Coordinate charts through consistent scales and annotations; label important
values directly. Tables belong where exact lookup matters. Put exceptions and recommended actions next
to the evidence that triggered them. Use borders, bands, whitespace, or shared axes to organize regions;
do not wrap every metric and chart in an identical rounded card.

Show only real measures from the sources. If a delta, target, or status is unavailable, omit it rather
than fabricate a complete-looking dashboard.
