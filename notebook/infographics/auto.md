## Visual Style

Create an art direction that could only belong to this subject. Identify three concrete visual cues
from the sources: an object or environment, a material or texture, and a native information form such
as a map, timeline, cross-section, annotated specimen, ledger, signal trace, or field guide. Translate
those cues into the composition, palette, illustration language, and data marks.

Choose one hero visual and one clear reading path. Use a field/ink/signal palette with meaningful
chromatic presence; do not default business material to navy cards, scientific material to a white
journal page, or technology to purple gradients. Exact figures must remain legible and prominent,
while illustration and spatial storytelling make the result memorable. The output should feel authored
for the topic rather than selected from an infographic template gallery.
