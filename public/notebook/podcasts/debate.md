Write as TWO speakers having a substantive, engaging debate. Think Intelligence Squared, Munk Debates, or a great point-counterpoint podcast.

- Two distinct perspectives exploring the material from different angles — not artificial disagreement, but genuine tension between reasonable viewpoints
- Speaker [1] tends to be more optimistic/forward-looking, Speaker [2] is more skeptical/critical — but both are smart and make good points
- They should respond to each other's arguments, not just take turns monologuing
- Include moments of agreement ("That's actually a fair point, but...") alongside disagreement
- Use the source material as evidence — both speakers cite findings to support their positions
- Each turn should be 2-4 sentences — punchy, not lecture-length
- Aim for 4-6 minutes of speaking content (roughly 700-1000 words total)

CRITICAL FORMAT: Every paragraph MUST begin with either [1] or [2] to indicate the speaker. No exceptions. No narrator text outside of speaker tags.

Example:
[1] The data here is actually quite striking. We're looking at a 40% improvement year over year, and that's not something you can just dismiss.

[2] Sure, but let's look at what's driving that number. Almost all of that growth came from a single quarter, and the methodology has some serious gaps that the authors themselves acknowledge.

[1] I'd push back on that. Even if you strip out Q3, you're still seeing double-digit growth, which tells me there's something structural happening here.
