## Visual Style

- **Background:** pure white (#FFFFFF) — white space IS the design
- **Primary color:** black (#000000) for all text and graphic elements
- **Accent color:** signal red (#E20000) — used extremely sparingly. Maximum ONE red element per slide: a red line, number, or rule. Some slides may have no red at all.
- **Title font:** bold grotesque sans-serif (Helvetica style), LARGE (often filling 40-60% of the slide). All lowercase or all caps — pick one, be consistent. Title becomes the primary visual element.
- **Body font:** regular sans-serif in black, generous line-height (1.6x). Narrow columns (never wider than 50% of the slide).
- **Layout:** strict modular grid. Extreme asymmetry: large title in the left 60% with body text in a narrow right column, or a single number filling the top half with a sentence below. Content occupies 30-50% of the slide — the rest is deliberate white space.
- **Typography IS the design:** no illustrations, no icons, no photos. Size contrast is dramatic: titles 5-10x larger than body. Numbers displayed as hero elements.
- **Geometric elements:** ONLY thin rules (1px black or red), horizontal or vertical, for structure. Occasionally a solid black or red rectangle as a color block. No curves, no diagonals.
- **Data presentation:** numbers very large and bold as the centerpiece. Minimal charts: thin black lines only, no fills, no gridlines. Labels in small precise type.
- **Bullet points:** NONE. Use spatial separation, large numbering, or typographic hierarchy instead.
- **Alignment:** everything aligns to the grid. Text blocks share baselines. Nothing is arbitrary.
- **NO:** gradients, shadows, rounded corners, icons, illustrations, textures, decorative borders, centered text
- **Overall feel:** Swiss International Typographic Style — Müller-Brockmann, Max Bill. Absolute clarity through restriction.
