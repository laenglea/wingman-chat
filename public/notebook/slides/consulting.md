## Visual Style

- **Background:** pure white (#FFFFFF) — clean, no textures, no gradients
- **Primary color:** deep navy (#1B2A4A) for titles, headers, and key data
- **Accent color:** muted teal (#2E8B8B) for chart fills, highlights, and emphasis
- **Secondary accent:** warm amber (#D4862F) — used very sparingly, maximum one amber element per slide for a "so what" callout
- **Tertiary:** light gray (#E8E8E8) for table backgrounds, supporting chart elements, and grid lines
- **Title font:** bold sans-serif, navy. Positioned top-left.
- **Body font:** regular sans-serif in dark gray (#333333), tight line-height
- **Layout:** rigid grid-based structure with mathematical precision. Heavy use of thin horizontal dividers (1px navy or gray lines). Dense, structured, intentional.
- **Key insight box:** each slide should have a bordered rectangle (navy border, light gray fill) summarizing the actionable takeaway, labeled "Key Takeaway" or "Implication" in small caps
- **Data presentation:** clean charts — horizontal bars, waterfall, stacked bars, line charts with data labels. Thin gridlines in light gray. Labels directly on bars (not legends). Use traffic light indicators for status.
- **Frameworks:** 2x2 matrices, pyramid diagrams, process flows with rectangular boxes and thin connecting arrows. Gray fills with navy borders.
- **Source citations:** small gray italic text at bottom-left
- **Bullet points:** em-dashes (—) or small navy squares, never circles
- **NO:** icons, illustrations, gradients, shadows, rounded corners, decorative elements
- **Overall feel:** McKinsey/BCG consulting style — structured, data-driven, every slide answers "so what?"
